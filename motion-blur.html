Understanding Motion Blur

By CineTorqueFilms • Shutter & Motion

Motion blur is not a mistake. It’s controlled chaos.

1. What Actually Causes Motion Blur

Motion blur happens when your shutter stays open long enough to capture movement. The longer the exposure, the more movement gets recorded.

This means blur is not random — it is directly tied to shutter speed.

2. The Shutter Speed Rule

For cinematic video, follow the 180° rule:

If you shoot at 24fps → use 1/48 shutter (or 1/50).

This creates natural motion blur that feels like film.

3. Too Fast vs Too Slow

Fast shutter (1/500): Sharp, crisp, but feels unnatural and harsh.

Slow shutter (1/10): Heavy blur, dreamy, but can destroy detail.

The sweet spot is balance — blur that suggests motion, not chaos.

4. Using Motion Blur Creatively

Motion blur can make speed feel real.

When shooting bikes, tracking shots with slight blur in the background create a sense of velocity that static images cannot.

5. My Approach

I don’t chase sharpness. I chase feeling.

If the shot feels alive, the blur is working.

If everything is perfectly sharp, it’s probably boring.
