Director's Commentary

Why this shot: We wanted to establish the rugged village terrain early on, contrasting the sleekness of the subject against the raw environment.

Camera Setup: Sony A7SIII on a DJI RS2 Gimbal. 35mm G-Master Lens.

Lighting: Natural sunlight, golden hour. We used a bounce reflector to fill the shadows on the rider.

Mistakes & Fixes: The initial pass was too bumpy. We fixed it in post using Warp Stabilizer and re-shot a tighter angle for safety.

Understand how this short film was built out of specific technical shots.

Establishing Shot

Setting the Scene

A wide angle to capture the environment and establish scope. Gives the audience spatial awareness before moving in close.

Tracking Shot

Following the Motion

Keeping the subject centered while moving parallel to them. This creates a high-energy, dynamic feel that matches the subject's speed.

Close-up

Focusing on Details

A tight shot on the machinery. Used to convey texture, mechanics, and raw power without environmental distraction.

Motion Shot

Fast Action

A fast-panning shot blurring the background. Requires a slower shutter speed mixed with ND filters to maintain proper exposure in daylight.
