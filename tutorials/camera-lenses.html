Camera Lenses Explained

Lenses control how a scene is captured. They affect field of view, compression, depth of field, and how a subject appears in a frame.

What is Focal Length?

Focal length determines how wide or zoomed-in an image appears. It is measured in millimeters (mm). Smaller numbers capture a wider view, while larger numbers zoom into the subject.

Common Lens Types

Wide Angle

16–35mm

Captures large environments and dramatic perspective.

Standard Lens

35–50mm

Produces a natural perspective similar to human vision.

Telephoto

70–200mm

Compresses distance and isolates subjects.
