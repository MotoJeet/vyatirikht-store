Camera Movement

Camera movement adds energy, emotion, and storytelling to a shot. Different types of movement can guide the viewer’s attention and create cinematic visuals.

Common Camera Movements

Pan

The camera rotates left or right on a fixed position.

Tilt

The camera moves up or down from a fixed position.

Dolly

The camera physically moves forward or backward.

Tracking Shot

The camera follows a moving subject.

Handheld

The camera is held by hand to create natural movement.

Crane Shot

The camera moves vertically using a crane or jib.

Why Movement Matters

Movement helps create emotion and immersion. A slow push-in can create tension, while a tracking shot can make the viewer feel like they are part of the action.
