Framing & Composition

Composition is the way elements are arranged inside a frame. Good composition guides the viewer’s eye and makes shots feel balanced and cinematic.

Basic Composition Rules

Rule of Thirds

Divide the frame into a 3×3 grid. Important subjects should be placed along the grid lines or intersections.

Leading Lines

Lines in the environment guide the viewer’s eye toward the subject. Roads, railings, and architecture work well for this.

Frame Within a Frame

Using objects like windows, doors, or trees to frame a subject creates depth and visual interest.

Symmetry

Symmetrical compositions create strong visual balance and dramatic shots.

Negative Space

Leaving empty space around a subject can make the frame feel minimal and powerful.

Foreground & Background

Adding foreground elements creates depth and a stronger three-dimensional look.

Why Composition Matters

A well-composed frame helps tell the story visually. Even simple scenes can become cinematic when the subject is placed thoughtfully within the frame.
